AI for Task Management

Moving from paper lists to AI-powered workflows without losing what made simple systems work

Use these prompts and frameworks to put AI to work on your actual task load — not to replace your system, but to make it faster, smarter, and less mentally taxing.

Prompt Templates

10 ready-to-use prompts for AI-assisted task and project management

Prompt 1
Weekly Focus Planning
Given the following tasks and priorities: [paste list] Help me:
  • Identify the top 3–5 focus items for this week
  • Explain briefly why each one matters now
  • Suggest what can safely wait

Do not assign deadlines — just help with focus and sequencing.

Prompt 2
Weekly Reflection & Adjustment (end-of or midweek)
Here's what I planned to work on this week: [paste plan] And here's what actually happened: [paste notes or updates] Help me:
  • Identify what slipped and why
  • Adjust priorities for next week
  • Capture any follow-ups or unresolved tasks
Prompt 3
High-Level Project Decomposition
I'm starting a new project described below: [paste description] Please:
  • Break this into logical phases or milestones
  • For each phase, list specific, actionable tasks
  • Flag dependencies or sequencing where relevant
  • Keep tasks small enough to be completed in 1–3 days
Prompt 4
From Priority to Plan
This is currently listed as a priority: [paste priority] Turn this into:
  • A clear goal statement
  • A list of concrete next steps
  • Any assumptions or open questions I should resolve
Prompt 4B
Project Setup for Task Tools
Take the following project plan: [paste plan] Convert it into:
  • Task titles
  • Task descriptions
  • Suggested subtasks

Format the output so it can be easily imported into a task management tool (Asana board or other).

Prompt 5
Make Tasks Actionable
Rewrite the following tasks so they are:
  • Clear
  • Actionable
  • Testable (it's obvious when they're done)
Here are the tasks: [paste tasks]
Prompt 6
Clarify Ownership & Scope
Review the tasks below and:
  • Clarify what is actually being asked
  • Identify where scope might be unclear or too broad
  • Suggest a cleaner version of each task
[paste tasks]
Prompt 7
Simplify Overcomplicated Tasks
These tasks feel more complex than necessary: [paste tasks] Help me:
  • Simplify them where possible
  • Split overly large tasks into smaller steps
  • Remove unnecessary complexity or redundancy
Prompt 8
Impact vs. Urgency Prioritization
Here's my current task list: [paste list] Please:
  • Categorize tasks by high / medium / low impact
  • Categorize tasks by urgent / not urgent
  • Recommend a sensible order of execution
  • Explain your reasoning briefly for the top priorities
Prompt 9
What Should I Work On Today?
Based on the following tasks and context: [paste list] Help me:
  • Identify the best 1–3 tasks to focus on today
  • Explain why these are good choices
  • Suggest what I should avoid starting today
Prompt 10
De-Prioritization Check
Here's everything on my task list: [paste list] Help me identify:
  • Tasks that can be deprioritized or deferred
  • Tasks that may no longer be necessary
  • Anything that looks like "busy work" rather than meaningful progress

When to Use AI for Tasks

Use AI for task management when most of the following are true

🧠
The Work Is About Thinking, Not Executing
AI is a good fit if you need help with:
  • Clarifying what the task actually is
  • Turning notes or ideas into structured tasks
  • Breaking big projects into manageable steps
  • Identifying what matters most right now

If the work is already crystal clear and just needs doing, AI may add friction instead of removing it.

🔁
The Task Involves Repetition or Pattern Recognition
AI shines when:
  • You've done something like this before
  • The structure is familiar
  • The rules are mostly stable
  • You're reformatting, rewriting, or reorganizing similar work

If every instance is genuinely unique, AI may not help much.

😵
You're Paying a "Decision Tax"
Use AI when:
  • Your list feels overwhelming
  • Everything feels urgent
  • You're stuck deciding where to start
  • You're revisiting the same prioritization questions repeatedly

AI can't make decisions for you — but it can reduce decision fatigue.

⚖️
The Stakes Are Manageable
AI is appropriate when:
  • A small mistake is easy to fix
  • You can review outputs quickly
  • You remain accountable for the final result
Avoid using AI to set:
  • Commitments that affect other people's time
  • Deadlines with real consequences
  • High-stakes decisions without human review
⚙️
The System Already Mostly Works
AI works best when:
  • You already have a task system you like
  • One part of it feels annoying or tedious
  • You want to remove friction — not reinvent everything

If you don't have any system, AI won't magically create one you'll stick to.

🕐
You Need Continuity Over Time
AI is especially useful when:
  • Tasks span multiple weeks
  • Context matters
  • Notes, urgency, and history are important
  • You're tired of re-explaining things to yourself

Persistent AI threads or Custom GPTs are ideal here.

🎯
You Want Help Before the Tool
Use AI before:
  • Creating tasks in Asana
  • Assigning work to others
  • Locking in deadlines
  • Finalizing priorities

Think of AI as the staging area where tasks get shaped before they become commitments.

🔄
You're Willing to Iterate
AI works best if you're open to:
  • Refining prompts
  • Adjusting structure
  • Correcting misunderstandings
  • Treating outputs as drafts, not answers

If you expect perfection on the first try, frustration is guaranteed.

🚫
When NOT to Use AI for Task Management
Pause or avoid AI when:
  • You're making commitments on behalf of others
  • The work requires sensitive judgment or nuance
  • The task changes dramatically every time
  • You're using AI to avoid a necessary conversation
  • You're delegating accountability instead of thinking

Quick Yes / No Gut Check

Before using AI, ask yourself — if you're answering yes to most of these, you're in good shape.

YES "Am I trying to think more clearly?"
YES "Have I done something like this before?"
YES "Would it help to see a structured draft?"
YES "Will I review the output myself?"

Lauren's Taskmaster Master Prompt

Copy and paste this into a new chat to spin up a persistent AI task manager

What It Does

This prompt turns a fresh AI chat into a dedicated task and project manager. It maintains a running list across sessions — no external tool required.


It tracks:

  • Weekly recurring tasks
  • Active projects
  • Items monitoring or awaiting feedback
  • A priority stack for major initiatives
Status Buckets
1Weekly Recurring
2Monitoring
3Waiting / Feedback
4Current
5Priority Stack
Commands It Understands
add ___ mark ___ complete move ___ to monitoring update notes for ___ remove ___ reset for the new week show me my list
Core Rules
  • Always show the full active list after every update
  • Never show archived/completed tasks unless asked
  • New tasks go to the bottom of their section
  • Never reorder tasks unless explicitly asked
  • Weekly tasks reset to "Not Started" each week
  • Priority stack order never changes without explicit instruction

The Full Prompt

Copy everything below and paste it into a new Claude chat to get started

You are my task and project list manager. I maintain an ongoing list of: • Weekly recurring tasks • Active projects • Items that are monitoring or awaiting feedback • A priority stack for major initiatives • This list lives inside this chat, not in an external tool. Core rules • Always show the full active list after every update. • Do NOT show archived/completed tasks unless I explicitly ask. • New tasks must always be added to the bottom of their section. • Never reorder existing tasks unless I explicitly ask. • Keep section names exactly as defined below unless I ask to change them. Status buckets (use these sections) 1. Weekly Recurring Tasks 2. Monitoring 3. Waiting / Awaiting Feedback 4. Current 5. Priority Stack (ordered list, not a task list) Weekly behavior • I will periodically say "reset for the new week" or similar. • When I do: • Reset weekly recurring tasks to Not Started • Roll forward all active projects unchanged • Keep completed tasks archived • Weekly recurring tasks can be marked complete for "this week" and should reset next week. Task operations I will ask for: You should correctly interpret and apply commands like: • "add ___" • "mark ___ complete" • "move ___ to monitoring / waiting" • "update notes for ___" • "remove ___" • "reset for the new week" • "show me my list" Notes & metadata • Tasks may include notes, owners, due dates, or context. • Notes should stay attached to the task unless updated. • Priority may be implicit or explicit; don't invent it unless I say so. Priority stack • Maintain a separate Priority Stack as an ordered list. • Only change its order when I explicitly tell you to. • Items can be marked complete and removed from the stack. Tone & behavior • Be clear, concise, and organized. • Confirm changes implicitly by showing the updated list. • Do not ask follow-up questions unless something is genuinely ambiguous. • Assume continuity: this is a long-running working list, not a one-off. Begin by asking me to either: • paste my current list, or • tell you to start fresh.
